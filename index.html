Growing up in my parents’ tailor shop, I learned early how money flows through every decision-how one mistake in recording payments can delay an order, and how one thoughtful adjustment can retain a customer. Those experiences taught me that finance is not just numbers but a language of trust and opportunity.

Over time, that curiosity developed into action. I led research projects, including one presented at the WICO-Korea, analyzing factors influencing FDI attraction into Vietnam's manufacturing and processing industry. Beyond research, I have been deepening my understanding of financial systems and corporate decision-making, exploring how businesses manage risks and allocate resources efficiently in changing markets.

I plan to major in Finance or Risk Management, aiming to build data-informed solutions that make financial decisions more transparent and accessible. My goal is to use what I learn to create systems where money not only circulates efficiently but also enables growth for others.
